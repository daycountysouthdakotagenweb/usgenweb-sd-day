FOURTH GENERATION

64. Martha Theona Edith STOPPEL (15) was born on 20 Dec 1917 in Rochester, MN. She was baptized on 25 Dec 1917 in Rochester, MN. She was confirmed on 25 Mar 1934 in Rochester, MN. She died on 12 Jun 1995 in Brampton, ND. She was buried on 16 Jun 1995 in Britton Cemetery, Britton,SD. She has reference number 1.1.5.1.1. Theona Edith Stoppel was born to Otto & Martha Stoppel in 1917. Her mother died in childbirth. The child (Martha) was abandoned by Otto and was raised to school age by her praternal grandparents, At this time she was passed around other relatives as a nanny/hired girl to whom ever could use her at the time. Aunt Laura (acted as guardian) & Rose & Ralph Stoppel were the persons who seemed to be in charge most of the years.

She was married to William P. BOSSE (son of Paul H. BOSSE and Elsie Augusta BRUMMOND) on 10 Dec 1937. At the home of Mr. & Mrs. Ralph Stoppel Rochester, MN. William P. BOSSE (15) was born on 1 Oct 1915 in Sargent Co. ND. He died on 10 Dec 1991 in Britton, SD. He was buried on 14 Dec 1991 in Britton Cemetery, Britton,SD. He was a Farm Laborer. William was a farm laborer for the Mayo brothers in Rochester, MN. On November 24, 1938, Donna Jean was born at Saint Marys Hospital to Bill and Martha Bosse. She was baptized on December 31. She married Charles M. Zimmer, Jr. (born December 28, 1937) on February 13, 1960, in Cincinnati, Ohio. They have four children: Thomas Charles, Virginia Edith, Laurel Jean, and Krista Rose.

Thomas Charles was born on November 25, 1960. He married Jennifer
Schmitz on October 6, 1984. They have two children, William Charles
(born December 13, 1986) and Janelle (born November 1988).

Virginia Edith was born on May 17, 1962. She married Chris Foltz on
November 2, 1985. They have one child, Jillian (born October 12, 1988).

Laurel Jean was born on April 6, 1964. She married Dan Herris on
October 3, 1989.

Krista Rose was born on December 14, 1965. She married Nicalas Nero on May 27, 1990.

On February 8, 1939, Bill and Martha sold their trailer home. Bill left for South Dakota on February 11, and Martha and Donna Jean went to South Dakota with Shirley, Ralph, Rosalie, Charles, and Russell on February 18. On March 8, Rosalie joined the Ladies Aid.
